About Me

As a dedicated PhD Physics scholar at the University of California, Merced, my focus is on Quantum Computing and Computational Physics. I have a strong background in Physics with a Master's degree from San Jose State University and Chandigarh University.

Research

Currently engaged in groundbreaking research on Frequency Modulated Quantum Rabi Array. I have a keen interest in the dynamics of spins, quantum computing, and numerical simulation techniques.

Projects and Research

Quantum Ising Model

Numerically solved the Quantum Ising Model with rigorous calculations, advancing understanding in quantum mechanics.

Oscillatory Edge Modes in 2D Spin-Torque Oscillator Arrays

Explored the dynamics of a 2D array of STOs, mapping it to a non-Hermitian SSH model, with significant findings in energy spectra and edge states.

Efficient Diagonalization of Sparse Hamiltonian Matrix

Developed a faster algorithm for exact diagonalization of Hamiltonian matrices in 2D STO arrays, enhancing computational efficiency in quantum computing simulations.

Frequency-Modulated Quantum Rabi Array

  • Investigated the Quantum Rabi model array, focusing on dynamics, entanglement properties, and energy spectra using Hamiltonian formulation.
  • Applied adiabatic time evolution for controlled transitions in quantum systems, ensuring maintenance of ground state, pivotal for quantum operations and simulations.
  • This project's insights are instrumental for understanding and manipulating quantum systems, particularly in the context of quantum computing.

Master's Thesis: Topology of a 2D Driven Spin Torque Oscillator Array

Conducted in-depth research on the topology of 2D STO arrays, contributing to the field of condensed matter physics.
