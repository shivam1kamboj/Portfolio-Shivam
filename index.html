About Me

I am a PhD candidate in quantum computing and computational physics at UC Merced (advisor: Prof. Lin Tian), graduating in Fall 2026. I work at the intersection of theory, experiment, and large-scale computation. My expertise is foundational and platform-agnostic: I treat a qubit as a two-level quantum system and a cavity as a bosonic mode, so the Hamiltonian-dynamics, open-systems, and quantum-control methods I use transfer directly across superconducting, atomic, and photonic hardware.

Three core pillars:

  • Hardware & Control: Hands-on with real devices. At LLNL I built pulse-level control and linear-inversion quantum process tomography (QPT) pipelines to drive and characterize superconducting qudit hardware — operating at the control and measurement layer.
  • Theory & Simulation at Scale: Rigorous physics modeling. I build large-scale sparse exact-diagonalization and open-quantum-system dynamics pipelines that run on SLURM/HPC clusters (Tian Group).
  • Software & Deployment: I ship working code people use — from a full-stack LLM application in production at UC Merced (Google Gemini API, GCP, Flask) to an open-source library for benchmarking quantum error correction under realistic noise (nonmarkov_qec).

I started as a theorist — modeling strongly coupled spin-cavity and cavity-cavity systems, writing custom numerical integrators for noisy open-system dynamics, and building sparse exact-diagonalization pipelines at HPC scale. At Lawrence Livermore National Lab, I closed the loop with hardware: I wrote the generalized Python control and analysis pipelines connecting gate-level control models to measured device behavior, including a modular linear-inversion QPT pipeline for rapid experimental feedback and gate-sequence fidelity validation. I am now deepening that hardware fluency through hands-on experimental work on superconducting hardware and chip design in the Sharping Lab.

The arc from Hamiltonian to simulation to a working experiment is what I care about most. I am seeking research and engineering roles where I can apply this end-to-end fluency in quantum modeling, control, and hardware to make quantum technology genuinely useful.

Current Research

My doctoral research with Prof. Lin Tian centers on the Quantum Rabi Dimer: coupled cavity–qubit systems in the ultrastrong-coupling regime. Using large-scale exact diagonalization and open-system dynamics on HPC clusters, I map the spectral structure, symmetry protection, and quantum phase behavior of these arrays and show how their ground-state structure supports deterministic Schrödinger-cat states and parity-protected, noise-biased bosonic qubits. These are the same theoretical structures that underlie superconducting transmon-resonator and bosonic-code platforms, so the work connects fundamental light-matter physics directly to the modeling foundations of next-generation quantum hardware. Two first-author manuscripts are in preparation, including a superconducting-circuit realization developed in discussion with experimental collaborators.

To support this research, I also built a private full-stack simulation interface for the model (Python/Flask backend, JavaScript visualization) — described in Software & Projects below. It is kept private to protect unpublished results.

Key Projects

nonmarkov_qec: Benchmarking Quantum Error Correction Under Non-Markovian Noise

Open-source library — v1 released

The Problem: Most QEC threshold studies assume Markovian (memoryless) noise, but real devices are dominated by temporally correlated, 1/f-type noise. nonmarkov_qec supplies reproducible, physics-grounded correlated-noise models for stress-testing error-correcting codes against the noise hardware actually exhibits.

  • Validated noise engine (v1): An Ornstein–Uhlenbeck (OU) trajectory sampler and a sum-of-OU approximation to 1/f noise, both numerically validated against their target spectral statistics.
  • Built for QEC pipelines: Designed to feed correlated-noise trajectories into stabilizer simulation and decoding (Stim / PyMatching) for surface-code threshold studies under non-Markovian noise — integration and threshold experiments in active development.
  • Tech Stack: Python, NumPy/SciPy, Stim, PyMatching.

View Repository on GitHub

High-Performance Algorithm: Sparse Matrix Diagonalization

The Problem: Standard NumPy routines were too slow for diagonalizing (250,000 x 250,000) Hamiltonian matrices in 2D STO arrays.

The Solution: Developed a custom fast-diagonalization algorithm optimized for sparse matrices.

The Impact: Reduced execution time from 3,500 seconds to 66 seconds (98% speedup).

Quantum Error Correction: Surface Code Simulation

  • Overview: A Python-based simulation of a distance-3 surface code, built to demonstrate foundational principles of quantum error correction.
  • Tech Stack: Python, NumPy.
  • Features: Initializes a grid of data qubits, introduces random bit-flip errors, measures stabilizers to generate error syndromes, and tracks error candidates via overlapping stabilizers to correct errors and restore the logical state.
